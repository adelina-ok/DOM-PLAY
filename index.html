SCENE III. A room in Polonius' house.

LAERTES: I'm tired and hungry and my sister is the favorite child. Is anyone out there?

OPHELIA: What's wrong with you?

LAERTES: Well, Hamlet is a guy I know. He's incredibly arrogant and dumb. So, why does he smell good?

OPHELIA: I see where you're coming from.

LAERTES: I have all these problems and I'm terrified. But I'll be careful, because there is safety in the feeling of fear.

OPHELIA: You bring up a good point. I hate Hamlet, too.

LAERTES: I need to go, my dad's looking for me.

Enter POLONIUS

LORD POLONIUS: You should smile more. Laertes, go to France and become a chief, there you will find friends and a husband.

LAERTES: I will do as you say.

LORD POLONIUS: No time like the present.

LAERTES: Bye Ophelia, don't forget what I said.

OPHELIA: I won't

LAERTES: OK, see you later.

EXIT
